Vocabulary Deep Dive: vulnerable

1. Arti Utama

Vulnerable (adjective) berarti mudah terluka, terancam, atau terpengaruh — bisa secara fisik, emosional, psikologis, maupun teknis (sistem keamanan). Akar katanya dari bahasa Latin vulnus = luka.

"Children are especially vulnerable to misinformation online."
→ Anak-anak sangat rentan terhadap informasi yang salah di internet.

2. Makna Tambahan

  • Emosional Berani menunjukkan sisi lemah diri sendiri — justru dianggap kekuatan dalam konteks kepemimpinan modern.
    "Being vulnerable with your team builds deeper trust."
    → Bersikap terbuka tentang kelemahan kepada tim membangun kepercayaan yang lebih dalam.
  • Keamanan IT Sistem atau kode yang memiliki celah keamanan.
    "The server was vulnerable to SQL injection attacks."
    → Server tersebut rentan terhadap serangan SQL injection.
  • Sosial Kelompok yang membutuhkan perlindungan ekstra.
    "We must protect the most vulnerable members of society."
    → Kita harus melindungi anggota masyarakat yang paling rentan.

3. Pola Penggunaan

  • Struktur: be vulnerable to + noun/gerund · feel vulnerable · make someone vulnerable
  • Collocations: emotionally vulnerable · vulnerable population · vulnerable system · feel vulnerable · leave someone vulnerable
  • Kapan digunakan: Saat mendeskripsikan risiko, keterbukaan emosional, atau kelemahan yang bisa dieksploitasi.

4. Nuansa & Perbedaan

  • Vulnerable vs. Weak: Weak = lemah secara umum. Vulnerable = spesifik pada potensi untuk dilukai/diserang — dan tidak selalu negatif (vulnerability = keberanian).
  • Vulnerable vs. Fragile: Fragile = mudah pecah/rusak secara fisik. Vulnerable lebih luas: fisik, emosi, sistem.

6. Contoh Kalimat (10 Contoh)

1."She felt vulnerable after sharing her personal story in front of the team."
→ Dia merasa rentan setelah berbagi cerita pribadinya di depan tim.
2."Elderly people are more vulnerable to severe complications from the flu."
→ Orang tua lebih rentan terhadap komplikasi serius akibat flu.
3."The legacy system is highly vulnerable to cyberattacks."
→ Sistem lama tersebut sangat rentan terhadap serangan siber.
4."Good leaders aren't afraid to be vulnerable with their team."
→ Pemimpin yang baik tidak takut untuk menunjukkan sisi lemah mereka kepada tim.
5."Small businesses are especially vulnerable during economic downturns."
→ Bisnis kecil sangat rentan selama penurunan ekonomi.
6."The data showed that vulnerable populations were hit hardest by the policy change."
→ Data menunjukkan bahwa kelompok rentan paling terdampak oleh perubahan kebijakan itu.
7."After the breakup, he felt emotionally vulnerable for months."
→ Setelah putus, dia merasa sangat rentan secara emosional selama berbulan-bulan.
8."Sharing your vulnerabilities can actually strengthen relationships."
→ Berbagi kelemahan justru bisa memperkuat hubungan.
9."The open API endpoint was left vulnerable without proper authentication."
→ Endpoint API terbuka itu dibiarkan tidak aman tanpa autentikasi yang tepat.
10."Being vulnerable doesn't mean being weak — it means being honest."
→ Menjadi rentan bukan berarti lemah — itu berarti jujur.

7. Kesalahan Umum

❌ Salah preposisi:

✗ "vulnerable for attacks"

✓ "vulnerable to attacks"

❌ Hanya dipakai untuk konteks negatif/fisik:

✗ Lupa bahwa "being vulnerable" (terbuka secara emosional) adalah konsep positif dalam leadership modern.

8. Latihan (Practice)

📝 Fill in the Blank:
  1. "The unpatched server is ________ to remote exploits." (vulnerable)
  2. "She felt emotionally ________ after the difficult conversation." (vulnerable)
  3. "We need to protect ________ communities from economic shocks." (vulnerable)
  4. "The startup is ________ to cash flow problems in its early stage." (vulnerable)
  5. "True leadership means being willing to be ________ with your team." (vulnerable)
🔄 Parafrase:
  1. "The system can easily be hacked." → "The system is vulnerable to hacking."
  2. "He showed his weaknesses openly to the team." → "He was vulnerable with his team."
  3. "Those people are at serious risk." → "Those are highly vulnerable populations."

9. Konteks Profesional

IT Security "Our penetration testing revealed several vulnerable endpoints that needed immediate patching."
→ Pengujian penetrasi kami mengungkap beberapa titik rentan yang perlu segera ditambal.
Leadership "The manager's willingness to be vulnerable about her own mistakes created a psychologically safe environment."
→ Kesediaan manajer untuk terbuka tentang kesalahannya menciptakan lingkungan yang aman secara psikologis.
Data/Compliance "Personal data of vulnerable individuals must be handled with extra layers of protection under GDPR."
→ Data pribadi individu yang rentan harus ditangani dengan lapisan perlindungan ekstra sesuai GDPR.

10. Speaking Booster

  1. "Honestly, I felt really vulnerable presenting that idea for the first time."
    → Jujur saja, saya merasa sangat rentan saat mempresentasikan ide itu untuk pertama kali.
  2. "We need to make sure our vulnerable users are protected."
    → Kita perlu memastikan pengguna yang rentan terlindungi.
  3. "That system is still vulnerable — we haven't patched it yet."
    → Sistem itu masih rentan — belum kita perbaiki.
  4. "Being vulnerable takes courage, especially in a professional setting."
    → Menjadi terbuka membutuhkan keberanian, apalagi di lingkungan profesional.
  5. "Small teams are often more vulnerable to burnout."
    → Tim kecil sering lebih rentan terhadap kelelahan kerja.

11. Grammar Insight

💡 "Vulnerable" selalu diikuti preposisi "to" — bukan "for", "of", atau "from".

be/feel/remain vulnerable + TO + noun/gerund

Bentuk kata benda: vulnerability (jamak: vulnerabilities)
"Security vulnerabilities must be documented and tracked."
